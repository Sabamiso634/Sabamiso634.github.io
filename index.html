Overview

Closed-loop coupling from M1 to body and back to S1

We coupled a GPU-based multi-region SNN with a mouse forearm musculoskeletal model to examine real-time closed-loop brain–body simulation.

The present loop connects M1 activity to antagonist extensor–flexor muscle activation, body motion, and S1 feedback. It provides a basis for future tests of sensory feedback, cerebellar interactions, and cortico–basal ganglia loops in movement generation.

Future hypothesis

Toward hierarchical reinforcement learning in freely moving virtual rodents

Future work will test a hierarchical reinforcement-learning hypothesis by implementing reinforcement-learning-inspired rules in cerebellar and cortico–basal ganglia loops.

The planned platform will extend from constrained forearm lever-pull control toward whole-body virtual rodents that can move freely and support closed-loop validation of neural circuit roles.

Neural model

Multi-region SNN including M1/S1, BG, TH, cerebellum, and CPG.

Closed loop

M1 → muscles → body → S1 feedback, coupled online with the mouse forearm model.

Expansion

From forearm lever-pull control toward freely moving virtual rodents for testing cerebellar and cortico–basal ganglia loops.
